ACCOMMODATION

Here you can find informations on how you can accommodate and live at uni:

1. University Dorms (Cheapest & Basic)

  • Most public schools (like UPT, Ion Mincu in București, UPT in Cluj etc.) have student dormitories on or near campus.
  • Rooms are usually shared with another student, with shared kitchen/bathroom spaces — and they’re very affordable compared to private rent. At UPT, dorms cost around ~€65/month and you pay a deposit on arrival.

  • You must apply early (often after admission) and follow university instructions to get assigned a place.

2. Private Student Residences

  • In larger cities like Barcelona or Madrid, private student apartments/residences offer single rooms with private bathrooms and modern facilities — more comfortable but pricier than dorms.
  • Good for students who want more privacy while studying architecture.Good for students who want more privacy while studying architecture.

3. Renting an Apartment / Shared Flat

  • Independent rental (sharing a flat/house) is common for older students, especially architecture students who need more workspace for models and projects.
  • Cost varies by city but gives more independence than dorms.

🧠 Tips for Architecture Students

  • Studios and apartments are often preferred after the first year because you’ll need more space for project work.
  • University dorms are great when you first arrive — cost-efficient and social.
